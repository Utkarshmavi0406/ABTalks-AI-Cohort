Member Claims Process Guide
This document explains how members file, track, and appeal claims under their coverage plan. This is a sample/synthetic document for training purposes only.
1. Filing a Claim
Most in-network claims are filed automatically by your provider. For out-of-network care, or if your provider does not file on your behalf, follow the steps below.
Complete the Member Claim Form, available on the member portal.
Attach an itemized bill from your provider, including procedure codes.
Attach proof of payment if you paid out of pocket.
Submit the claim within 90 days of the date of service.
Submit via the member portal, mobile app, or mail to the address on your member ID card.
2. Claim Statuses
Pending — Claim has been received and is under review. Typical review time is 10-14 business days.
Approved — Claim has been processed and payment has been issued to the provider or member.
Denied — Claim was not approved for payment. A denial letter with the specific reason is mailed and posted to the member portal.
Partially Approved — Some services on the claim were approved and others were denied or reduced.
3. Common Reasons for Denial
Service not covered under the member's plan.
Prior authorization was required but not obtained.
Claim was filed after the 90-day deadline.
Provider is out-of-network and the plan does not cover out-of-network care.
Missing or incomplete documentation.
4. How to Appeal a Denied Claim
Members have the right to appeal a denied or partially approved claim. Appeals must be submitted in writing within 180 days of the denial notice.
Submit a written appeal request, including your member ID and claim number.
Include any supporting documentation, such as a letter of medical necessity from your provider.
The plan will respond to standard appeals within 30 days.
Expedited appeals (urgent medical situations) are reviewed within 72 hours.
If the internal appeal is denied, members may request an external review by an independent third party.
